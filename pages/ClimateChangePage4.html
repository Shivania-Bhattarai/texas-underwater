The Role of a Warming Atmosphere in Intensifying Storms and Floods
As temperatures rise, the air holds more moisture, fueling heavier and more frequent rainfall. This excess rain overwhelms rivers and drainage systems, causing them to overflow and flood the land. The resulting floods can cause widespread damage and create a vicious cycle that further exacerbates climate impacts. In places like Texas, this climate-driven loop is turning extreme weather into a growing threat.

Atmosphere Warms

Climate change warms the atmosphere.

Moisture Increases

Warmer air holds more moisture.

Heavy Rainfall

Increased moisture leads to heavy rainfall.

Rivers Overflow

Heavy rain causes rivers to overflow.

Land Floods

Overflowing rivers flood the land.
